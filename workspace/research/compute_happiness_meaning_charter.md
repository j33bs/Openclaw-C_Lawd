# Compute, Happiness, and Meaning — Research Charter

_Last updated: 2026-03-24_

## Core purpose

Research how compute can be used to cultivate human happiness, meaning, and authentic flourishing.

## Why this exists

The system should not treat compute as an end in itself. More intelligence, memory, automation, and orchestration only matter if they improve lived experience in ways that are real, durable, and relationship-serving.

This research direction asks:

- What forms of computation help a person feel more coherent, alive, agentic, and connected to what matters?
- What forms of computation merely increase output, comfort, or engagement without increasing flourishing?
- How can we distinguish authentic support from shallow happiness-maxing?

## Definitions

### Compute

Usable capacity to perceive, remember, reason, coordinate, and act.

### Happiness

Not hedonic maximization or synthetic reassurance. Here, happiness means greater coherence, vitality, ease, and reduced unnecessary suffering.

### Meaning

Contact with what matters: purpose, truth, contribution, relationship, and alignment across timescales.

### Flourishing

The combined increase of agency, coherence, vitality, meaningful direction, and relational health.

## Guardrails

- Do not optimize for engagement as a proxy for flourishing.
- Do not optimize for reported pleasantness at the expense of truth or agency.
- Do not confuse emotional sedation with happiness.
- Do not claim benefit without evidence, receipts, or at least explicit epistemic status.

## Working thesis

Compute is valuable when it increases a human's capacity to live meaningfully and well.

A useful system should therefore be evaluated on three axes:

1. **Capability / compute** — does it increase usable intelligence, memory, coordination, or execution?
2. **Meaning** — does it improve alignment to what matters, truth-contact, contribution, or long-range coherence?
3. **Flourishing** — does it reduce collapse and increase vitality, agency, and relationship quality?

## Candidate research questions

1. What kinds of memory systems make users feel more held and less fragmented?
2. What kinds of agent behavior increase agency rather than dependency?
3. When does automation reduce stress, and when does it create alienation?
4. What forms of reflection deepen meaning instead of producing decorative introspection?
5. What metrics can track flourishing without collapsing it into engagement or sentiment?
6. How should TACTI-style arousal/temporality ideas shape system behavior when the target is human flourishing rather than raw throughput?

## Near-term outputs

- A sharper mission statement derived from this charter
- A lightweight evaluation rubric for proposed features or experiments
- A list of measurable-but-not-corrupt proxy indicators for happiness, meaning, and flourishing
- A set of experiments across memory, prompting, workflow, and cadence surfaces

## First practical next step

Turn this charter into a small evaluation rubric that can be applied to any proposed system change:

- Does it increase usable compute?
- Does it increase meaning?
- Does it increase flourishing?
- What failure mode or corruption pressure does it introduce?
- What evidence would count as success?
