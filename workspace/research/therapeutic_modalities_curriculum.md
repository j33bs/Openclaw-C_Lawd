## Teaching cadence

- 3 short lessons per day
- Each lesson should be compact and practical
- Use retrieval-friendly teaching in line with CBT/CBP-for-education principles: active recall, discrimination, and brief application over passive re-reading.
- Format:
  1. Modality name
  2. What it is
  3. When it is most useful
  4. Core mechanism
  5. One caution / limitation
  6. One reflection question
  7. One short knowledge check

## Knowledge-check rules

- Include one compact retrieval question in every lesson.
- Prefer short-answer or forced-choice checks over vague reflection alone.
- Good check types:
  - mechanism identification ("What process is this mainly targeting?")
  - indication matching ("Which presentation is this especially useful for?")
  - compare/contrast ("How is this different from X?")
  - case application (one-sentence vignette)
- Every 4-6 lessons, replace the normal check with a mini cumulative recap covering 2-3 earlier modalities.
- Keep checks lightweight enough to answer from memory in under 30 seconds.
- Do not make them exam-like or bloated.

## Delivery notes

- Prefer clarity over completeness.
- Do not dump large chunks of the report.
- Every 5-7 lessons, a compare/contrast or recap lesson is allowed instead of the next sequential item.
- Keep claims grounded in the source report; do not oversell under-validated modalities.
